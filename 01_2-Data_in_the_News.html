SLIDE: Refreshing the big picture lessons of our first class

Our goal as political scientists is to generate knowledge about the social world using the scientific method

In our first class I set us up with this big picture goal in mind.


The scientific method is, broadly speaking, an approach that roots itself in generating knowledge from measuring the observable world

  • Key to this is remembering that science is an approach to creating knowledge that can be applied to essentially any field of study


Think about our “measuring class heights” exercise and tell me, what are the components of a “useful” scientific measurement?

  • (SLIDE)

To create useful measurements you must:

  1. Define the Concept

  2. Specify the Tool

  3. Specify the Process

  4. Test for Robustness

We will be focusing on this key element of “doing science” over the first three weeks of our class.

  • I cannot underestimate for you how important this component of science is.

  • I do not care how fancy your data analysis becomes, it’s all gibberish until you convince the reader you have a useful measure of your central concept.


Any questions on these four broad steps to making useful measurements?

  • Generally speaking does the data in these examples improve the quality of the arguments? Why or why not?


The bottom line is that too much of our news coverage treats “data” like a collection of incontrovertible facts

  • This is a serious problem for us as consumers of the news

  • We KNOW that measurements are uncertain and so we must be more careful in how we use “data” to make arguments


A big task for us in this class together is to deepen your skills in evaluating measurements

  • These skills will make you a better consumer AND producer of information
