Our goal as political scientists is to generate knowledge about the social world using the scientific method

Read slide


Remember!

  • Science is the process of testing ideas using empirical data

  • Empirical data is generated by measuring the world

  • All measurements include uncertainty

  • Therefore, all knowledge is uncertain and needs to be improved!


That’s the job we’ve signed up for in this discipline

  • In this class we develop our measurement skills in order to help us quantify the uncertainty AND work to make it smaller

  • That means our skills from this semester directly translate into improving our knowledge


This week we’ll develop and practice our measurement skills by exploring two data projects

SLIDE: Ok, but what do I mean by R vs RStudio?

R is the statistics engine

Key analogy: R is the engine and RStudio is the fancy dashboard we use to drive the car

  • When you open RStudio, you are opening R!

  • RStudio just makes it much, much easier to work with R to get things done.


You can absolutely do everything in R without RStudio.

  • You cannot use RStudio without having R.


So, this semester you are learning to code in R but we’ll be working in RStudio.

  • Make sense?


Posit Cloud is handy as a starting point because it bundles both together with nothing to install!
