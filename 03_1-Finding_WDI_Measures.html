Section 1: Developing a Measurement

In our first week of class we introduced the basic concepts we use to talk about measurement


REVEAL: Your first step is to produce a conceptual definition

  • e.g. “clearly describes the concept’s measurable properties and specifies the unit of analysis”

  • Chapter 1 of our book gave us a nice template for creating conceptual definitions

  • The concept of X is defined as the extent to which Y exhibit the characteristic of Z


REVEAL: Your second step is to convert your conceptual definition into an operational definition

  • Some books refer to this as operationalization or instrumentalization

  • In either case, the job is to describe the instrument you will use to measure the concept

  • This could be numbers on a ruler, a 0 to 100 feeling thermometer or a Likert scale set of ordinal categories


REVEAL: Technically this is beyond the treatment presented in the first chapter of the textbook, however, this is also a very important step in measuring the empirical world

SLIDE*: Evaluating a measurement

Section 1: Evaluating a Measurement

That leads to the conceptual tools we use for evaluating a measure


By validity we mean how closely the measure comes to the intended concept (without unintended characteristics)

  • The textbook introduced us to face validity and construct validity as two ways to evaluate the validity of a measurement


By reliability we mean how reliable a measure is.

  • Assuming no change in the property being measured, a reliable measure is one that gives the same result each time it is applied to the same case


While both are important qualities, validity tends to be the ball game for us

  • If you’re not measuring the actual concept you aimed for then the data isn’t creating knowledge for us


Importantly, each of the measurement steps on the previous slide introduce their own complications in terms of validity and reliability

  • A weak or idiosyncratic conceptual definition will almost certainly weaken your validity

  • A poorly constructed operational definition will almost certainly hurt both your validity and reliability!
